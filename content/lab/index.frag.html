The Lab collects public experiments that are meant to be used or inspected: interactive toys, source-trail notes, and repo-backed tool writeups.

Toys: interactive notes with a working demo, concrete parameters, and a public source trail.

Tools: small public software extractions with a real repository, bounded API, and deployed lineage.

Current toys include Bug in a Rug, Chew Dither Lab, Sine Dot Grid, Stroke Chime, Native Triangle vs. NES-Style Triangle, Timing vs. Spacing, Anticipation Before Action, Phosphor, Falling Hall, and Dead Beat.

Current tools include ChewGumDitheringGun v0.0.1, ChewGumTimeChime v0.1.0 and chewgum-dsp v0.1.0.

New lab pages need a working artifact or public source trail, a bounded claim, and review before they become high-signal pages in sitemap or LLM guidance.
